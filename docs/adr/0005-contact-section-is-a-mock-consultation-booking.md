# The Contact section is a two-panel mock consultation booking, not a contact form

The homepage's `#contact` section is the destination of the Navbar's primary CTA, but the brand already lets a visitor reach a specific human in better-targeted ways: every Agent carries a phone and social links, and every Property Detail Modal has an agent sidebar with a "Book Consultation" button (decorative until now - see ADR 0003). A generic "Get In Touch" form dropped here would duplicate those paths and add nothing. Rather than a contact form, we're making this section a **Consultation Booking**: a mock, single-screen flow for requesting a consultation. There is no backend - it is theater - so the design's job is to *feel* like a real booking, not to actually send or store anything.

The layout is two panels on one screen, no popups. The left panel is a scrollable image-row Property selector (thumbnail, name, city, price), with a pinned "No specific property - general enquiry" row at the top. The right panel is a month calendar: pick a day, then a row of time slots for that day appears, then the visitor's name and email, then a confirm action. Picking a Property is **optional**; selecting one auto-fills its Agent (via the existing `Property.agentSlug`) into an agent chip at the top of the right panel, so the visitor sees the real person they're "booking with". With no Property selected, the flow is a general enquiry and the chip falls back to a **Lead Agent** - a designated real Agent (the highest-profile one, Siriporn Thanawan, chosen so the choice is documented and overridable rather than a magic string) rather than a neutral brand placeholder, keeping a credible human on every path. Confirming swaps the right panel inline to a success state naming the agent, date, and time; because there is no popup, that resolution happens in place. Confirm is enabled once a date, a time, a name, and a valid email are present - the Property stays optional throughout.

We considered the obvious alternatives and rejected them. A plain lead-capture form was the premise we started from and discarded: it duplicates the per-agent and per-property contact affordances the site already has, and adds no reason to exist. Requiring a Property before booking would give a always-real agent and a single clean path, but it walls off everyone who arrives from the Navbar CTA with no property in mind - the majority of that traffic. A neutral "Reel Estates Team" placeholder for the general path was tempting (no fake human), but a named senior agent reads as more trustworthy and the roster already exists. We kept the calendar at date **and** time-slot fidelity (rather than date-only or part-of-day) because a "consultation" with no time reads as unfinished, and the extra step is cheap.

The practical effect: `#contact` is no longer a placeholder or a form-to-build-later - it is a real (if backend-less) booking surface, and it is the natural funnel floor the rest of the site can point at (see ADR 0006 for the deep-linking that makes that concrete). The anchor id stays `#contact` so existing links and the Navbar CTA keep working, even though the section is a booking, not a contact form.
